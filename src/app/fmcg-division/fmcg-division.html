Sales and Rental Analysis

Scenario:

Global Nexus rents and sells costumes in Samarkand. Products include Halloween costumes, traditional costumes, and festival outfits. The company wants to:

  1. Analyze rental frequency across stores.
  2. Understand customer preferences and satisfaction.
  3. Forecast costume demand for upcoming festivals.
  4. Make stocking and pricing decisions.

Step 7: Inferential Statistics

Forecasting Halloween Rentals Next Week

Sample (n=5): 12, 15, 10, 8, 14

95% Confidence Interval:
CI = 11.8 ± 1.96 × (2.95 / √5)
= 11.8 ± 2.59
= [9.21, 14.39] units per store

Interpretation: Expected rentals next week: 9–14 units per store.

Step 8: Questionnaire & Survey Insight

Sample Questions:

  1. Rate costume variety (1–5)
  2. Rate rental experience (1–5)
  3. Would you rent again? (Yes/No)

Survey Results (n=10): Average rating = 4.3 → High satisfaction.

Step 9: Business Decisions

  • Stock Halloween costumes higher for the upcoming festival.
  • Traditional costumes need moderate inventory.
  • Festival costumes require limited stock.
  • Use survey feedback to improve service quality.
